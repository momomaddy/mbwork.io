The Challenge

The problem wasn't awareness.
It was legitimacy.

In the wake of intense public and market scrutiny, Adani Group launched a national brand platform — a 100-second TVC developed by Ogilvy India, celebrating 35 years of service and anchoring the Group's identity in resilience, execution, and nation-building.

The immediate challenge was not visibility. It was coherence. The Group had diversified into independently governed, market-leading businesses across energy, logistics, airports, materials, and direct-to-consumer — each with its own audience, tone of voice, and credibility expectations. A singular corporate statement, however powerful on television, risked fracturing the moment it touched each vertical.

"The real problem was not: how do we promote a campaign? It was: how do we shift public perception when attention is high and sentiment is volatile?"

Traditional PR rebuttal wasn't sufficient. Every message carried reputational risk. The opportunity was to transform a broadcast moment into a sustained, believable public narrative — one that felt consistent at group level while remaining authentic to each business unit.

My Role & Approach

Four pillars of execution.

My role was to convert a television campaign into a live communication system operating across paid, earned, and owned media simultaneously. This required four distinct workstreams running in parallel.

01
Tone Adaptation
Translated the core campaign voice into the natural language of each business unit. Infrastructure emphasised operational reliability. Airports focused on passenger experience. Consumer products communicated everyday quality. Same idea — different expression for each audience.
02
Real-Time Narrative Management
Built a social command centre running continuous listening and sentiment analysis. Identified narrative inflection points and adapted messaging live. Not content scheduling — newsroom-mindset applied to brand communication.
03
Decentralised Storytelling
Expanded into 10+ vernacular ecosystems. Activated Tier 2–3 creators. Developed storytelling platforms — Ports of Progress, #Parivartan — that let the narrative be carried by communities, not just the brand.
04
Cross-Business Alignment
Reviewed and aligned communication across 10+ business units, working with internal teams and agency partners. Every operational update, service announcement, and digital story was calibrated to reinforce the platform rather than compete with it.

Why This Campaign Matters
HKKDH demonstrates three capabilities operating under pressure: strategic thinking when stakes are high and information is incomplete; narrative architecture that connects business, culture, and public sentiment at scale; and an adaptive, feedback-driven communication system that predates — and mirrors — the logic of AI-native marketing. This was not campaign execution. It was reputation infrastructure.
