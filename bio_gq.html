Chapter 01
The work behind the work
I sold to people. Before I built campaigns, I answered customer questions.


At sixteen, five friends and I pooled our annual trip money, borrowed the rest from my father and opened a video games parlour in our hometown. Town's top business guy opened one for his son too and eventually KO'ed us. But looking back, we lost the business. And started a culture. Three years later, I travelled through the remote villages in Kashmir selling educational books and toys to schools.

At 21, I was manning customer service for a McDonalds store with the highest footfall in the city. A year later, I was doing the same at Waitrose in Edinburgh.
Jobs taught me something no strategy deck ever could: people rarely tell you exactly what they need, but they almost always show you.


In college, I was elected President of the Students' Association. Turned it into a revenue generator by creating and selling college hoodies inspired by our hockey and drama societies.

In early 2012, I was the Project Lead & Director for "It Gets Better Campus Campaign" - the campus-wide adaptation of the global 'It Gets Better' movement, designing a localized narrative strategy to foster inclusivity and support for LGBTQ+ youth on campus.

Later that year, I was responsible for facilitating workshops & focus group for Police Scotland's #WeCanStopIt campaign. I worked with schools, youth clubs, colleges & Black and Minority Ethnic communities to help shape campaign messaging.
My favourite project though, I was paid five pounds, was to art direct the college prospectus. And somewhere in between, college took us across Europe. We wandered through museums and galleries, toured Coca-Cola factories and whisky distilleries, and learnt that every great product comes wrapped in a story.


So in 2016, I finally started my corporate gig and what a gig it has turned out to be...

Today, I am as interested in internal systems as external campaigns. I am currently designing a leadership dashboard that connects brand, communications, social media and PR to business outcomes, making it easier to understand not just what worked, but why. More recently, I helped reposition a Y Combinator startup from an AI agent marketplace to "hire digital employees that work."

Beyond work...
I bake croissants and muffins on occasion. Ski when there's snow.
